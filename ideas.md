# Design Ideas for Matt Clarke | Independent AI Research

## Context
A personal research publishing hub for 3 articles on AI safety, architecture, and human-AI collaboration. Audience: AI researchers and developers. Tone: serious, independent, intellectually rigorous.

<idea>
**Design Movement:** Swiss International Typographic Style meets Technical Manifesto

**Core Principles:**
1. Typography as architecture — every heading is a structural element, not decoration
2. Negative space as signal — silence between ideas gives them weight
3. Monochromatic restraint with a single sharp accent (deep amber/ochre)
4. Grid rigidity broken by deliberate asymmetry in section layouts

**Color Philosophy:**
- Background: near-white warm paper (#F7F5F0) — evokes academic print
- Text: near-black charcoal (#1A1A1A)
- Accent: deep amber (#C47D0E) — signals urgency, intellectual fire
- Muted: warm grey (#8C8070) for metadata and secondary text

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Asymmetric two-column layouts: narrow left column for labels/metadata, wide right for content
- Article cards as horizontal bands, not grid tiles
- Hero: full-width typographic statement, no image — text IS the visual

**Signature Elements:**
1. Thin horizontal rules (1px amber) as section dividers
2. Large pull-quotes in oversized italic serif, offset to the right margin
3. Article numbering in large faded numerals behind content

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Hover reveals underline in amber on article titles
- Smooth scroll with section anchors
- Comments section styled as a formal response ledger

**Animation:**
- Entrance: text lines fade up with 40ms stagger, no bounce
- Hover: 200ms color transitions only, no scale or movement
- Scroll: subtle parallax on hero text only

**Typography System:**
- Display: Playfair Display (serif, bold 700) — gravitas and authority
- Body: Source Serif 4 (serif, regular 400) — academic readability
- Mono/labels: JetBrains Mono — for citations, dates, metadata
</idea>

**Design Movement:** Dark Academic / Research Terminal

**Core Principles:**
1. Deep dark backgrounds evoking late-night research sessions
2. Phosphor-green or electric-blue accent — the glow of a monitor in a dark room
3. Dense information hierarchy — this is a researcher's workspace, not a blog
4. Structural lines and borders as organizing elements

**Color Philosophy:**
- Background: deep navy-black (#0D1117) — GitHub-dark inspired
- Surface: #161B22 for cards
- Accent: electric teal (#00E5CC) — signals cutting-edge, forward-thinking
- Text: #E6EDF3 (primary), #8B949E (secondary)

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Full-width dark hero with glowing text and subtle grid overlay
- Articles listed in a dense research-index style with metadata columns
- Sidebar navigation for article sections

**Signature Elements:**
1. Subtle dot-grid or circuit-board texture on hero background
2. Glowing border-left accent on blockquotes
3. Code-like monospaced labels for dates and categories

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Hover: teal glow effect on cards
- Active states with bright accent borders
- Comments styled as a terminal/console interface

**Animation:**
- Hero: typewriter effect on subtitle
- Cards: slide-in from left on scroll
- Transitions: fast 150ms, sharp easing

**Typography System:**
- Display: Space Grotesk (geometric sans, bold) — technical authority
- Body: IBM Plex Serif (serif) — readable, technical

## Selected Design: Dark Academic / Research Terminal

Deep navy-black background, electric teal accents, IBM Plex Serif for body text, Space Grotesk for display. Dense research-index layout with a glowing hero. This design signals that this is serious, forward-thinking, independent research — exactly the tone needed to reach AI researchers and developers.
